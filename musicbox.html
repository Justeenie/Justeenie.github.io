Laser Cutting and its applications

Laser Cutting is a very efficient and effective way of cutting many pieces of a material such as acrylic, wood paper and many more. With its highly accurate cutting and impressive speed, its only real downside is that it can only cut 2D flat objects. This is not a huge limitation considering the fact that a 3D model such as a box, could easily be divided into 6 flat sides with tabs (small extrusions on each side) to later assemble.

Model of the Laser Cutter in school

How to laser cut

Laser cutting is actually pretty simple. Just have a 2D sketch ready (it could be a 2D view of a flat 3D model piece) and export it to CorelDraw and position the pieces together closely on one area of the canvas (in this case, it would be the area of the plywood/acrylic). I was taught to always keep the pieces tightly fitted and close to one another on the canvas in order to furthur maximize the amount of useful area for future cuts. The colours of the lines on CorelDraw matter, red for cutting and green for engraving. Last but not least, the lines should all have 'hairline' thickness.

Moving on, all that's left is to select the "jog" option and use the joystick to move the laser cutter to the starting position at the top-left corner of the board. The red button that looks similar to a star enables a laser pointer to help with the positioning of the starting point.

My laser cutting assignment

For my assignment, I was given the task to make a music box that has a movable lid with at least one engraving on it as a decoration. I decided to take it one step further and come up with a design slightly more unique! Since its a music box, I decided to make the box in a shape of a piano and engrave the keys of the piano as decoration. Below I will show you the process of how I designed my Music Box.

Using Fusion360 for 3D Modeling

Before diving into the design of the music box, I needed to make the design parametric. It is simply defining parameters using variables and then proceeding to dimension the model using these variables, in that way, if I wanted to change the dimensions of the box I could easily change a single variable. One such situation where I had to utilize this was when I modelled using the thickness '3mm' but later had to change the thickness to '3.9mm' as that was the thickness of the plywood provided to me.

To laser cut flat, 2D pieces of wood and later assemble it into a 3D box, the simple approach would be divide the box into 6 flat sides (assuming its a 6 sided box), then later assemble it and glue it together. One issue is that the contact surface area for gluing the sides together maybe too small, therefore to deal with this I was taught to make tabs on the sides of the box that connects with other sides to interlock the sides.

For the sides of the box I need to make a 'hinge' that I could attatch the lid to and allow it to rotate for my lid to open and close. And to my suprise later on, the laser cut circles were basically perfect! I needed 2 of these, one for each side of the box so I utilized the mirrror feature to repeat this on the other side of the box.

Personally, the lid was the most challenging part to design. Just a flat lid with one extrusion on each side to attatch the lid to the hinge could not possibly be that challenging right? That's absolutely correct! It was only challenging for me due to me being paranoid of the small hinge snapping or the hinge getting loose after a period of time so I wanted to avoid literally fitting a square peg into a round hole. Instead I decided to design an extrusion slightly larger than the hinge and later on manually file it down to a circular shape.

The rest of the model are basically the repeat of above to make the stair like shape of my music box. I attatched all the sides at the top to the lid so that i could later fit the music crank inside without obstructions.

Fusion360 Model of my Music Box

Now, all that was left was to export it CorelDraw where I can now add engravings onto it as decoration and start the laser cutting process. Exporting is simply done by selecting the sketch of a body, and exporting it as a DXF file, this gives a 2D sketch which is dimensioned and would allow it to be imported to CorelDraw and cut by the machine.

engravings are easily added by taking a picture and pasting it onto the canvas. The machine is able to engrave black and white images very clearly but even if the image is coloured, the machine will just use varying amounts of power and frequency to at least give it a differing shade. In my case, I chose to engrave some piano keys as well as some other decorations.
